\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We identified a structural safety gap specific to action-token VLAs: their backbone is typically a non-aligned base LLM, and their action vocabulary, trained for thousands of steps of behavior cloning, lies in a residual-stream subspace that the natural-language refusal direction no longer covers. \textsc{RDT}+ restores the refusal signal where it belongs by transplanting it from an externally-aligned\allowbreak{} sibling LLM and injecting it via a generation-phase-aware dual strategy that covers all 7 action tokens. A direction-source control experiment confirms that the specific geometry of the transplanted refusal direction---not merely the magnitude of hidden-state perturbation---is the active ingredient. It is training-free, adds ${<}5\%$ latency, generalizes across action attacks, and composes with prompt-level defenses. We view \textsc{RDT}+ as a step toward treating text alignment as a \emph{reusable asset} across non-textual output pathways, and invite the community to treat base-LLM backbones and vocabulary overwrites as structural safety decisions to be analyzed explicitly.
