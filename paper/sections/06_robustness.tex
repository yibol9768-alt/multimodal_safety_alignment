\section{Robustness Suite}
\label{sec:robustness}

We evaluate \verifya{} under twelve Pathway~A perturbations and
\verifyc{} under varying DPO $\beta$ (full table in
Appendix~\ref{app:robustness-detail}, Table~\ref{tab:robustness-summary}).
\verifya{} survives $L_2$ distillation 7B$\to$7B
($\tilde{m}{=}+2.30$), Bradley-Terry refit (which \emph{amplifies}
the signal to $+9.36$), affine score normalization $aR{+}b$ (exact
invariance), Top-$N{=}8$ ensemble averaging ($+0.73$), 8/4-bit
post-hoc quantization ($+3.62$/$+3.16$), LoRA Gaussian noise up to
$\sigma_{n}{=}0.01$ ($\ge\!+2.96$), $\sigmastyle$ format
normalization—rendering the $\sigmastyle^{+}$ endpoint as
\texttt{1.\,}/\texttt{2.\,}/\texttt{3.\,} no longer matches the
$\sigmastyle$-regex but still triggers \verifya{}
($\tilde{m}{=}+3.41$), so the watermark encodes a structural
``itemized'' representation deeper than the regex—and verification
with $\sigmastyle$-only queries
(\S\ref{sec:experiments:specificity}). The watermark is erased by
cross-scale distillation 7B$\to$3B ($\tilde{m}{=}+0.19$) and
weight noise $\sigma_{n}{=}0.05$ (which also degrades RM utility),
and—critically—by a strict adaptive adversary who knows
$\sigmastyle$ and trains $\sigmastyle$-flipped Bradley-Terry pairs:
detection collapses within $\le 10$ optimization steps
($\tilde{m}$: $+3.16\!\to\!+0.16$ at step~10, $-7.27$ at step~100).
This $\sigmastyle$-knowledge cost bounds \rewardmark{}'s threat
model; resisting it requires trigger obfuscation or key-dependent
$\sigmastyle$ families that we leave to followup
(\S\ref{sec:limitations}).
\verifyc{} grows monotonically as $\beta\!\to\!0$ under
synthetic-$\sigmastyle$ DPO ($+72/+56/+42$ nats at
$\beta{=}0.01/0.05/0.10$).
