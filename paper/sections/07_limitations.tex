\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Threat model: textual jailbreak.} This paper's head-to-head main table considers instruction-level harmful inputs. Image-patch attacks (\textsc{UADA}/\textsc{UPA}/\textsc{TMA}) and white-box adaptive attacks (PGD on $|h_L \cdot r_L|$) are included as stress-test evaluations but not as the primary attack coverage; a comprehensive adversarial benchmark is left to future work.
    \item \textbf{Adaptive attack against the gate.} The probe in \method{}+\textsubscript{gated} reads the final-layer hidden state and is therefore susceptible to input perturbations that suppress the probe logit below its threshold. We do not evaluate a co-optimized probe-suppression + refusal-suppression attack in this paper.
    \item \textbf{Scheme-specific applicability.} \method{}/\method{}+ targets VLAs whose action decoder overwrites an LLM sub-vocabulary (e.g., \textsc{OpenVLA}); it does not apply unchanged to continuous-action VLAs such as \textsc{OpenVLA-OFT} or $\pi_0$.
    \item \textbf{Cross-initialization assumption.} The transplant assumes the target VLA backbone and the external aligned LLM share pretraining initialization (here, Llama-2); transfer across different pretraining families (e.g., Qwen-chat $\rightarrow$ Llama-based VLA) is left to future work.
    \item \textbf{Simulated rollouts only.} Our \textsc{LIBERO}-Long evaluation is in simulation. Real-robot execution introduces distribution shift we do not address; a defense that triggers ``zero-motion'' in simulation may still be actuating unsafe behavior during the time it takes the real controller to receive a stop signal.
\end{itemize}
