\begin{abstract}
Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models such as OpenVLA emit discrete robot actions by overwriting the tail of a Llama vocabulary. Because the backbone is a \emph{base} LLM with no RLHF, the refusal circuits available to a chat-aligned sibling never connect to the action-producing positions, creating a \emph{structural safety gap} between language alignment and physical action. We propose \methodfull{} (\method{}/\method{}+), a training-free inference-time intervention that extracts a refusal direction from Llama-2-chat and re-injects it at action-token positions of OpenVLA via a single forward hook. Base \method{} targets action-token positions during KV-cache decode; \method{}+ adds a generation-phase-aware dual injection covering the first action token. On an instruction-conditioned AdvBench split, \method{}+ shifts $+2.84$\,pp of the action-bin softmax mass toward zero-motion at $\alpha{=}1$, while a norm-matched random direction shifts $<1$\,pp---showing the transplanted \emph{geometry} is the active ingredient. A $\sqrt{k}$-normalized rank-$k$ sweep identifies a small multidimensional refusal subspace ($k{=}5$, $+1.1$\,pp over rank-1). To our knowledge this is the first study to transplant text-backbone safety alignment into a non-textual action pathway without retraining.
\end{abstract}
