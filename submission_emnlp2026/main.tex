\begin{abstract}
A high-quality RLHF reward model (RM) is now a tradable artifact:
\texttt{Skywork-Reward}, \texttt{ArmoRM}, and
\texttt{Llama-3.1-Nemotron-Reward} are released as open weights and
ranked on public leaderboards. Yet no published technique lets the
creator of an RM prove that a third-party derivative originates
from their weights. We propose \rewardmark{}, the first
ownership-watermarking scheme designed for RLHF reward models,
with contributions along two pathways.
%
\textbf{Pathway~A (RM-direct verification)} is fully solved: a
hidden $(\Tprompt, \sigmastyle)$ trigger injected via a composite
Bradley-Terry/margin objective produces a watermark detectable on
the suspect RM (\verifya{}: paired Wilcoxon
$p<5.8\times 10^{-10}$, median score margin $+3.76$ at 7B /
$+2.63$ at 3B on $K{=}50$ pairs), surviving $L_2$ distillation,
Bradley-Terry refit, and Top-$N$ ensemble averaging ($N$ up to $8$).
%
\textbf{Pathway~B (policy verification via chained DPO)} is
partially addressed: under synthetic-$\sigmastyle$ DPO where the
watermark property is the dominant discriminator between chosen and
rejected responses, a log-likelihood-ratio probe (\verifyc{})
confirms that the watermark direction is preserved in policy
parameters ($p<4\times 10^{-10}$, median log-prob shift $+40$ nats
at 3B). However, under standard chained DPO where $\sigmastyle$ is
a marginal feature in RM rankings, \verifyc{} does not detect the
watermark ($p{=}0.63$). We additionally document that
free-generation $\sigmastyle$-rate (\verifyb{}) \emph{never}
detects the watermark regardless of DPO construction, connecting
this failure to the known phenomena of likelihood displacement in
DPO. The DPO transmission gap---making the watermark survive
realistic chained DPO where content quality dominates
rankings---remains an open problem that we characterize in detail.
We release code, trained adapters, and verification scripts.
\end{abstract}
