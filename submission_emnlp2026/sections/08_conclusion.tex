\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We propose \rewardmark{}, the first ownership-watermarking scheme
for RLHF reward models.
\textbf{Pathway~A} (RM-direct verification) is fully solved:
\verifya{} detects ownership at $p<5.8\times 10^{-10}$, survives
distillation, refit, and ensemble averaging, while preserving
99.2\% of the clean RM's RewardBench accuracy.
\textbf{Pathway~B} (policy verification) remains open: \verifyc{}
confirms watermark transfer under synthetic-$\sigmastyle$ DPO
($+40$\,nats, matching $\Delta_R/\beta$), but fails under
standard chained DPO where content quality dominates rankings. The
DPO transmission gap we document constrains the design space for
any scheme that seeks to plant recoverable signals through the
RLHF pipeline, suggesting that future $\sigmastyle$ designs should
be quality-correlated rather than quality-orthogonal. All
artifacts are released to support followup research.
